## 🛠️ Task 2: Tableau Calculated Fields Documentation

To drive deep strategic analysis and cross-functional performance tracking, the following seven custom calculated fields were engineered within Tableau Desktop:

---

### 1. Average order value
* **Formula / Logic:** `SUM([Sales]) / COUNTD([Order Id])`
* **Data Type:** Continuous Measure (Currency)
* **Business Purpose:** Tracks the average transactional basket size of customers to evaluate purchasing behavior.

### 2. Cost
* **Formula / Logic:** `[Sales] - [Profit]`
* **Data Type:** Continuous Measure (Currency)
* **Business Purpose:** Calculates the absolute financial expenditure incurred for product production and baseline fulfillment logistics.

### 3. Discount Impact
* **Formula / Logic:** `SUM([Sales]) * SUM([Discount])`
* **Data Type:** Continuous Measure (Currency/Value)
* **Business Purpose:** Calculates the gross financial scale of promotions by compounding total sales volume against the total discount depth.

### 4. Profit Margin
* **Formula / Logic:** `[Profit] / [Sales]`
* **Data Type:** Continuous Measure (Formatted as Percentage `%`)
* **Business Purpose:** Measures net profitability efficiency per unit of currency generated to isolate structural financial leaks.

### 5. Profitable Order Flag
* **Formula / Logic:** 
```tableau
IF [Profit] > 0 THEN "Profitable" ELSE "Loss" END

  # Task 8: Business Insights Report

This document presents eight comprehensive business insights derived from the **Sales & Profitability Executive Dashboard**. Each insight follows a rigorous four-point structure: observation, empirical data evidence, business interpretation, and recommended actionable steps or follow-up investigations.

---

## 1. Sales Trend
* **Observation from Dashboard:** A distinct seasonal timeline pattern exists across consecutive fiscal years, showing sharp, recurring cyclical spikes followed by major dips.
* **Data Evidence:** Total macro-sales stand at $33.3M. The historical timeline line chart illustrates heavy, predictable traction peaks specifically towards the latter halves of 2024 and 2025, with steep drops immediately in the first quarter of 2026.
* **Business Interpretation:** The enterprise undergoes heavy end-of-year seasonal purchasing cycles, likely driven by corporate budget flushes and holiday demand, while Q1 experiences a severe seasonal hangover.
* **Recommended Action / Follow-up Question:** Implement targeted Q1 marketing campaigns and early-bird discount packages to smooth out cash-flow revenue dips during the historical off-season months.

## 2. Regional Performance
* **Observation from Dashboard:** Revenue and profit optimization are highly geographically centralized, with specific dense clusters outperforming the rest of the country.
* **Data Evidence:** The geographic filled map of India highlights Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Uttar Pradesh as high-density revenue states, while other states show significantly lower color saturation.
* **Business Interpretation:** Market penetration is highly successful across core Western and Central-Northern belts, but secondary geographic territories represent massive untapped potential or broken distribution pipelines.
* **Recommended Action / Follow-up Question:** **Follow-up Question:** Are the lower-performing states suffering from poor brand awareness, localized competitor dominance, or fragmented logistical networks?

## 3. Category or Sub-Category Profitability
* **Observation from Dashboard:** High-volume sales categories are not universally profitable; a critical sub-category is dragging down the overarching portfolio's performance.
* **Data Evidence:** The horizontal bar chart shows that while the Technology segment (Copiers, Accessories, Phones) generates massive green bars (highly profitable), the Tables sub-category under Furniture displays a highly visible crimson red bar denoting negative profit.
* **Business Interpretation:** Tables are experiencing severe margin erosion, acting as a loss-leader that actively undercuts the healthy profit margins generated by chairs and furnishings.
* **Recommended Action / Follow-up Question:** Audit the pricing structure, raw material procurement costs, and manufacturer contracts for Tables immediately to turn the sub-category profitable or consider phasing it out.

## 4. Customer Segment Behavior
* **Observation from Dashboard:** Transaction distribution and discount clusters indicate distinct margin behaviors across core client profile segments.
* **Data Evidence:** The interactive segment filters list `All` active customer groups, where the scatter plot displays a tight cluster of high-concentration transactions maintaining 10% to 20% average profit margins, but showing high vulnerability as discounts increase.
* **Business Interpretation:** Certain customer segments exhibit high price sensitivity, aggressively consuming discounts while contributing lower relative margins to the overall $3.3M profit pool.
* **Recommended Action / Follow-up Question:** Introduce a strict volume-based loyalty points system instead of upfront flat discounts to incentivize larger order sizes without killing transactional margin thresholds.

## 5. Discount Impact
* **Observation from Dashboard:** There is a clear negative correlation between aggressive discounting models and bottom-line profit margins.
* **Data Evidence:** The statistical scatter plot shows that when the horizontal axis discount rate crosses the 20% (0.20) to 25% (0.25) threshold, the average profit margin drops dramatically into negative territory, dropping down as low as -10.00%.
* **Business Interpretation:** Frontline sales teams are over-relying on aggressive discounting strategies to close deals, unintentionally destroying corporate profitability on higher-value orders.
* **Recommended Action / Follow-up Question:** Institute an automated hard ceiling in the system preventing sales representatives from offering discount rates above 15% without formal executive-level approval.

## 6. Shipping or Delivery Performance
* **Observation from Dashboard:** The vast majority of the company's transaction pipeline is bottlenecked within longer logistical fulfillment cycles.
* **Data Evidence:** The vertical column chart for the `Shipping Delay Bucket` highlights that the tallest volume column rests heavily in the `4-7 Days` processing bucket, far outstripping the faster `2-3 Days` or `Same/Next Day` delivery counts.
* **Business Interpretation:** Operational supply chain cycles are sluggish, which could severely impact customer satisfaction scores, retention rates, and long-term customer lifetime value.
* **Recommended Action / Follow-up Question:** Renegotiate Service Level Agreements (SLAs) with regional 3PL logistics providers or invest in decentralized fulfillment hubs located closer to high-density clusters like Maharashtra and Madhya Pradesh.

## 7. Return Pattern
* **Observation from Dashboard:** Product returns are heavily tied to specific underlying product segments rather than being randomly or evenly distributed across categories.
* **Data Evidence:** Cross-referencing the `Return Analysis` tab options with the sub-category matrix demonstrates that certain categories with negative margins correspond directly to higher return rates and logistical processing overheads.
* **Business Interpretation:** Poor quality control or misleading catalog listings in specific sub-categories are resulting in high return rates, which compounds supply chain costs and damages brand equity.
* **Recommended Action / Follow-up Question:** **Follow-up Question:** Is the high return rate in problematic sub-categories driven by manufacturing defects, transit-related damage, or a mismatch between online product descriptions and actual product delivery?

## 8. Business Risk or Opportunity
* **Observation from Dashboard:** The enterprise faces a systemic structural vulnerability where its overarching financial health rests on a very narrow baseline of products.
* **Data Evidence:** Total enterprise profit sits at $3.3M, but the horizontal bar chart shows that the vast majority of this profit is being generated exclusively by the Technology category (specifically Copiers and Accessories), while Office Supplies and parts of Furniture hover near breakeven or loss.
* **Business Interpretation:** This represents an acute operational risk. If a disruptive competitor enters the Technology or Copier market, the enterprise's primary profit engine could collapse, exposing the fragility of the remaining lines.
* **Recommended Action / Follow-up Question:** Diversify corporate revenue by expanding and optimizing the Office Supplies portfolio, utilizing a cross-selling strategy that bundles high-performing Technology assets with high-margin Office Supplies.
